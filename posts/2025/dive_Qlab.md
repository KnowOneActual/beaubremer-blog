## Get started with QLab: your new best friend for show control

Hey everyone! Ever wonder how live shows, plays, or presentations switch music, video, and lights? The magic behind the
scenes is often thanks to a program called QLab.

Professionals and hobbyists use QLab to make precise cue lists. Do not let the software scare you. It is easy to learn,
free to start, and improves your projects.

Today, we will look at QLab and learn how to add music cues. Get ready to set up your first cues.

### Getting started with QLab

Download QLab from the Figure 53 website. The free version offers many audio features, making it ideal for beginners.

Once you open QLab, you will see a clean workspace. Use this workspace to build your cue list: a chronological list of
every action in your show.

### Quick tutorial: adding music cues in QLab

Let's add some music!

#### 1. Open your workspace

When you open QLab, a blank workspace appears. This workspace is your canvas.

#### 2. Drag and drop your audio

To add music, drag an audio file from your computer folders into the QLab window. You can use WAV, AIFF, or MP3 files.

- **Pro Tip:** Uncompressed files like WAV or AIFF work best. However, MP3 files work well for simple setups.

#### 3. Meet your first cue

Dragging in an audio file creates a cue. Each cue has a number for its position and a name based on the file.

#### 4. The inspector – your control panel

Below the list, you will see the Inspector panel. The Inspector acts as your control hub. When you click a cue, the
panel shows its settings.

- Audio cues have tabs for Levels (volume), Time & Loops (times), and Audio (routing).

#### 5. Setting playback

- **Volume:** In the Levels tab, use the fader to set the volume. You can add fades later. For now, keep it static.
- **Start and End Times:** In the Time & Loops tab, set when the audio starts and ends. This is useful to play a
  specific section of a song. Drag the markers or type in the times.

#### 6. Playing your cue

- **Go Button:** Click the Go button or press the Spacebar to play the selected cue.
- **Stop Button:** Click the Stop button next to the Go button to stop the sound.

### What's next?

These steps cover only the basics. Learning to manage audio cues is a great start. Next, you can explore:

- **Fades:** Make your music fade in and out smoothly.
- **Triggers:** How to start cues automatically or link them together.
- **Hotkeys:** Keyboard shortcuts for quick playback.
- **Groups:** Organizing cues together.

Although QLab may seem complex, practice makes it easy to use. The tool gives you precise control over your live events.

Happy cueing!
