We have all seen it online or heard it in meetings. Just when you thought the "chess, not checkers" cliché had died, its
successor arrives: the claim of playing "5D chess."

They boast of playing across time and alternate worlds, far beyond thinking a few moves ahead. They want you to believe
their strategy unfolds in hidden spaces.

It is a fun sci-fi concept, but a meaningless climb. It makes the original phrase look absurd.

### Complexity for its own sake

In a previous post,
[The Tell-Tale Cliché: Why Chess, Not Checkers Misses the Mark](https://blog.beaubremer.com/posts/are_you_playing_chess_v2/),
I made the case for checkers. Its simple rules hide deep strategy. The beauty of checkers or chess is in mastering
complexity within a clear system.

The "5D chess" boast does the opposite. It suggests that genius lies in making a system confusing, rather than mastering
it. The goal becomes complexity for its own sake.

Adding more dimensions makes the game impossible to play. Real strategy focuses on making smart decisions in a messy
reality.

### The tell-tale sign of a non-strategist

What does this boast tell us? When someone claims to play 5D chess, they do the opposite of a real strategist.

- A true strategist seeks clarity. They cut through noise to find the best path.
- The "5D chess" player creates noise. They wrap their ideas in jargon to seem smart.

This claim is a clever smokescreen. It avoids defending a plan by hiding in nonsense. How can you question a strategy in
another dimension?

Ultimately, this is the same move as looking down on checkers. It uses a catchphrase to claim genius without proof. It
is an attempt to win by declaring a superior game that no one else can see.

Real strategy is hard enough in our own world. It takes focus, discipline, and a deep understanding of the rules. The
most effective people master the reality we share instead of inventing new ones.

Let others have their multiverses. The best way to win is to play the board in front of you.
