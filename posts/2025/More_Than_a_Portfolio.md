I put off creating a personal website for a long time. My resume and LinkedIn profile seemed enough. Building a site
felt like too much work for few viewers. Was the effort worth it?

Building a site is worth the effort for other reasons. Its true value goes beyond the final product. The main message is
what building it says about you.

### **It shows you're willing to figure things out**

Building a site means stepping into the unknown. I remember the joy of learning serverless functions. The jargon can be
hard, but for a moment, I felt like I could hack the mainframe.

This process mirrors how we solve problems at work. It shows curiosity and a drive to learn. Both traits help when tools
change constantly.

### **Perfection isn't the point**

Your site can be simple. My own design goal is basic: the buttons work. This shows you get things done despite a skill
gap.

Waiting to become an expert holds us back. Perfectionism often looks like caution. Initiative means finding answers
along the way.

### **You prove you can follow through**

Launching a site is exciting. Maintaining it requires care.

A messy garage makes people lose trust. A clean shop builds confidence. Your website sends the same signal.

Updating your bio or projects shows steady care. This shows you finish what you start and take pride in your work. A
hiring manager sees this activity and knows you can lead projects. Likewise, a customer trusts a mechanic with a clean
shop.

### **It signals a builder's mindset**

Ultimately, these points show the difference between perfectionists and builders. Perfectionists wait for the right
tools, ideas, and timing. Builders start with what they have and make something.

Your website is proof of this builder's mindset. **Your site is a working prototype of your work self.**

A prototype is useful because it exists. Building one proves you can bring an idea to life. This shows you can get
things done.

Which person would you hire: the one still planning, or the one who built a working solution?

Your site answers that question before you speak. It is more than a link in your signature; it is the story of how you
work.
