Every event audio tech knows the feeling. The show starts in minutes, and you need to record a last-minute "Voice of
God" (VOG) announcement. Do not panic. You can capture a clean, professional VOG on-site using a basic interface, a
laptop with Logic Pro, and a Shure SM58 microphone.

## Setting the scene: your impromptu vocal booth

First, find a good recording location. Bustling event spaces make this hard, but you must minimize background noise and
room reflections.

Look for a space that absorbs sound instead of letting it bounce. Find a small, quiet room away from the main event
floor. A storage closet, an office, or a quiet corner can work well.

Once you find a spot, build a makeshift vocal booth. Collect soft materials like coats, blankets, or stage draping.

Drape these materials over mic stands or chairs to form a small enclosure. This setup absorbs voice reflections and
blocks ambient noise.

## Gear up: the essentials for a clean take

Next, set up your gear.

### Mic technique with the SM58

The Shure SM58 works well for live vocals. Its cardioid pickup pattern helps in noisy environments. The mic picks up
sound from the front while rejecting noise from the sides and rear.

Have the speaker hold the microphone four to six inches from their mouth. This distance provides a full sound without
sounding boomy. If they get too close, the proximity effect adds muddy low frequencies. Tell the speaker to talk
directly into the top of the mic.

### The all-important pop filter

A pop filter prevents harsh plosive sounds like "p" and "b". If you do not have one, you can make one using common event
items:

- **The sock method:** Pull a clean, thin dress sock over the microphone grille.
- **The coat hanger and pantyhose:** Bend a wire coat hanger into a circle. Stretch pantyhose over the frame. Attach
  this to the mic stand with gaffer tape.

## Recording and polishing in Logic Pro

Now, open Logic Pro to start recording.

### Setting up your session

1. **Create a new audio track:** Open a new project and add an audio track.
2. **Set your input:** Set the track input to your USB audio interface.
3. **Check your levels:** Ask the speaker to test the volume. Watch the audio meter. Keep the levels in the green and
   yellow zones. Peaks should only briefly touch the orange zone. Avoid the red zone to prevent distortion.

### A quick and effective effects chain

Next, polish the recording. Logic Pro's built-in plugins can quickly create a professional sound. You can start with a
vocal preset from the Library to load a pre-configured effects chain.

You can also build your own effects chain:

- **EQ (Channel EQ):** Use a high-pass filter set to 80-100 Hz. This removes low-end rumble without thinning the voice.
- **DeEsser:** Use this plugin to soften harsh "s" sounds. Start with a preset and adjust the threshold.
- **Compressor:** This evens out the volume. Use the stock compressor with a vocal preset. Aim for a few decibels of
  gain reduction.
- **Limiter:** Place a limiter at the end of the chain. This boosts the overall volume while preventing clipping.

Finally, bounce the track as a high-quality WAV or MP3 file. With these quick steps, you can deliver a polished
recording under tight deadlines.
